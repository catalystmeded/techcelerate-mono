## ASRT SPONSOR TYPE: GENERAL SPONSOR

**TechCElerate is a General Sponsor** — a commercial CE platform selling self-learning courses to individual RTs. This was confirmed in a prior ASRT call.

**Institutional Provider (IP) is NOT applicable.** IP is exclusively for health care institutions (hospitals, clinics, imaging centers) providing CE attended only by their own employees. TechCElerate sells to external customers — IP does not apply and was never an option.

**What General Sponsor means operationally:**
- No annual agreement fee — pay per-course submission only
- Each course submitted individually using the self-learning fee schedule
- No batch annual agreement — each submission is independent
- Priority handling available at 50% surcharge (vs. $45 flat for IPs)
- Same 9-week review timeline, same quality standards

## ASRT REVIEW PROCESS

### Timeline
- Standard review: **Up to 9 weeks** (varies by submission volume)
- Rush review: **10 business days** (50% surcharge — costly for General Sponsor)
- Busy seasons: Spring and Fall (universities in session — higher volume)
- Best time to submit: **Summer** (slower volume, faster turnaround)
- **⚠️ Critical path: Submit by April 25, 2026 for July 4 launch**

### What ASRT Reviews
1. Content accuracy and clinical relevance
2. Learning objectives (measurable, linked to content and assessment)
3. Author qualifications
4. Post-test quality (question format, difficulty, LO alignment)
5. Reference currency and formatting
6. Required components present
7. Word count vs. credit claim

### Submission Requirements
- Completed application form
- Course content (Word document format required for review)
- Post-test with answer key
- Author credentials/CV
- Learning objectives

## CQR (CONTINUING QUALIFICATIONS REQUIREMENTS)

### What CQR Is
- 10-year cycle for RTs credentialed after January 1, 2011
- Requires structured education in specific content categories
- Categories defined by ARRT for each discipline (Radiography, CT, MRI, Mammo, etc.)
- Different from standard biennium CE — more targeted

### CQR Content Categories (Radiography Example)
From ARRT Content Specifications:
1. **Patient Care** — safety, communication, assessment
2. **Safety** — radiation protection, equipment operation
3. **Image Production** — technical factors, positioning, quality
4. **Procedures** — diagnostic procedures by body system

## RISK: AI-GENERATED CONTENT AND ASRT

### Unknown Policy Area
ASRT's current policies were written before AI-generated content was prevalent. Key questions:

1. Does ASRT have a policy on AI-generated or AI-assisted CE content?
2. Does the author listed need to have personally written the content, or can they be the clinical reviewer?
3. If AI drafts and a licensed RT reviews/approves, does this meet ASRT standards?

### Mitigation Strategy
- **Always list the SME reviewer as the author** (they are clinically responsible for content accuracy)
- The AI pipeline is a "tool" used in the creation process, similar to research assistants, ghostwriters, and editorial teams
- Frame as: "Author: [SME Name], [Credentials] | Developed with AI-assisted research and writing tools, reviewed and approved by the listed author"
- If ASRT requires disclosure, be transparent — don't hide the AI involvement
- **Ask ASRT directly during re-contact call** — get their position on record
