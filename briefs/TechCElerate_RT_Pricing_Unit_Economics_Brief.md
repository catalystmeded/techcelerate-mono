### Market Pricing Summary
- **Budget tier**: $3-8 per credit (per-course purchase: TakeCE, FastCECredits, X-RayCE individual)
- **Value tier**: $44.99-$60/yr unlimited (GetYourCEU, eRADIMAGING, X-RayCE annual, Medical Professionals)
- **Premium tier**: $125-$340/yr (ASRT membership — includes non-CE benefits)
- **Legacy/textbook tier**: $55-$170 per course package (ScrubsCE, Gage CE)

### Key Insight
The market has clearly bifurcated: the legacy providers (ScrubsCE, Gage CE) still sell textbook-based per-course packages at $55-$170, while modern digital providers have converged on $45-$60/yr unlimited subscriptions. ASRT sits above at $125+ but bundles association membership benefits.

**Nobody is at $19/yr.** The lowest unlimited annual subscription is GetYourCEU at $44.99/yr. A $19/yr price would be 58% below the cheapest competitor.

## STRATEGIC PRICING QUESTIONS TO RESOLVE

### 1. Subscription model: DECIDED — Annual unlimited subscription only at launch
Single option, one price, one button, one decision. Per-course and per-credit options deferred to Phase 2 (priced to make annual the obvious better deal, e.g., $8/course when annual is $39).

**Launch timing: DECIDED — July 4th is LAUNCH day (open, not close)**

Site goes live for soft launch ~May 15. Free course + email list building starts June 1. Founding tiers GO LIVE July 4th. Tiers close on rolling schedule through summer (Aug 4 / Aug 18 / Sep 1 or sellout, whichever first). This provides buffer for ASRT approval, beta testing, and polish — July 4th is a marketing event, not a technical deadline.

**Tier close structure decision needed:**

| Approach | Description | Pros | Cons |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Staggered close** | Red closes Aug 4, White closes Aug 18, Blue closes Sep 1 | Rolling urgency. Each close = marketing moment. "Red SOLD OUT" drives White/Blue. | More complex to manage. |
| **Simultaneous close** | All three close same date (Aug 31 or Sep 1) | Simpler. One final deadline. | Less urgency per tier. |
| **Sellout-only** | No close date — each tier closes when 250 sell | Maximum simplicity. No arbitrary deadlines. | Could drag. No time-based urgency. |

**Urgency mechanics:**
- Live counter on site per tier: "187 of 250 Red spots remaining"
- Countdown timer to each tier's close date
- Email sequences: "Red tier is 80% full" → "Red tier SOLD OUT — White still available" → "Last chance: Blue tier closes in 48 hours"
- Each tier sellout is a social media moment and email blast trigger
- Pre-launch email list gets "Founding tiers go live July 4th — be first in line" countdown sequence

1. Blended revenue per subscriber across all tiers in Year 1, Year 2, Year 3?
2. At what year does a $250 Red lifetime member become unprofitable vs. annual subscriber at $39.99? (Break-even = ~6.3 years)

4. Staggered close vs. simultaneous vs. sellout-only?

8. What happens to the $25 extended offer if we haven't hit target subscriber count by Dec 31? Extend into 2027 or hard cutoff to standard $39.99?

### 3. Free tier / lead magnet: DECIDED — One permanently free course
Single, always-available free 1-credit course. Requires registration (email + name + profession for CRM). Serves as: quality proof, email capture, SEO bait ("free RT CE credit"). Prominent CTA on every page: "Get Your Free CE Credit."

"Free Course Friday" concept retired in favor of permanent free course (simpler to market, clearer value prop).

### 4. Student / new grad pricing: DECIDED — Free Year 1
Free for first year with .edu email or graduation date within 12 months. Captures new RTs before they develop competitor loyalty. Convert to paid at standard rate in Year 2. Even at 20% conversion rate, LTV is wildly profitable over a 40-year career.

Model: How many new RTs enter the workforce annually? What's the conversion rate assumption? What's the LTV impact?

### 5. Referral + Affiliate programs: DECIDED — Both via AffiliateWP (already owned)
- **Referral tier** (subscriber-to-subscriber): Friend gets 50% off Year 1, referrer gets $5 credit or free month. 
- **Affiliate tier** (external promoters): 20-30% commission ($8-10/conversion at $39/yr). Target: RT program faculty, department leads, RT influencers.
- Both managed through AffiliateWP Ultimate (lifetime license, already owned).

### 6. Refund policy: DECIDED — 7-day window with completion cap
"100% refund within 7 days of purchase, provided no more than 2 courses have been completed. After 2 course completions or 7 days (whichever comes first), all sales are final."
Fully automated via Stripe — system checks days since purchase + courses completed. Zero human involvement.

### 7. Price anchoring against ASRT?
- ASRT membership is $125/yr for 17 CE credits (effectively $7.35/credit)
- At $49/yr for unlimited, TechCElerate is dramatically cheaper per credit
- At $9.99 founding member, it's almost free by comparison
- Marketing angle: "All the CE credits you need for less than [fraction] of ASRT membership"
- Membership page: show value stack (Hormozi Grand Slam Offer) totaling $300+ in value, crossed out, show actual price

### 8. Annual vs. monthly billing: DECIDED — Annual only at launch
Market standard for RT CE is annual (biennium cycle is 2 years). Simpler, lower churn, better cash flow. Monthly option deferred to Phase 2 if data suggests demand.

### 9. B2B pricing (hospitals, imaging centers, clinics)
Deferred to Phase 2 (post-DTC launch). See `TechCElerate_RT_B2B_Enterprise_Brief.md`.

### 10. Minimum course count at launch

- Minimum viable: 6-8 courses covering 24+ credits (one full biennium requirement)
- Ideal: 15-20 courses covering 30+ credits with CQR coverage

## ARRT CREDIT REPORTING FEATURE (Competitive Differentiator)

Build into user dashboard: "Report My Credits" button that auto-generates:
- Copy-pasteable text block with every ARRT-required field per completed course
- Single PDF "CE Summary Report" (all completed courses in one table)
- Direct link to ARRT online reporting portal
- Step-by-step screenshots

Also: capture birth month at registration → auto-generate biennium deadline → iCal reminder download for 90/60/30 days before deadline.

No competitor does this well. eRADIMAGING shows completion history but doesn't pre-fill ARRT reporting. TakeCE provides good certificates but no auto-generator. This is a zero-cost differentiator (built from existing LearnDash + Uncanny data).

## AI COURSE UPDATE PIPELINE (Affects Unit Economics)

Annual course refresh cycle:
1. Claude scans each course against current clinical guidelines, publications, regulatory changes
2. Generates "Update Report" per course: what changed, suggested revisions
3. SME reviewer approves/edits (15-30 min per course)
4. Updated course pushed to LMS via API
5. ASRT re-review if substantive changes (50% of original submission fee)

Cost to keep 20-course library current: ~$3,000-$6,000/yr (vs. $40,000-$120,000/yr traditional).
This dramatically improves unit economics at scale — build into the model.
