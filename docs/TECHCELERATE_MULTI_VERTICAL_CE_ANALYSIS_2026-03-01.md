## MARKET QUALIFIER FRAMEWORK

Before ranking, here are the 10 criteria that determine whether a CE vertical is worth entering. Each scored 1-5.

| # | Qualifier | What It Measures | Why It Matters |
|---|-----------|-----------------|---------------|
| 1 | **Market Size** | # of licensed professionals | Total addressable learners |
| 2 | **CE Hours Required** | Annual mandatory hours | Guaranteed recurring demand per person |
| 3 | **Total Annual CE Credits Consumed** | Size × hours | The actual market volume |
| 4 | **Accreditation Difficulty** | How hard to become an approved CE provider | Barrier to entry (for you AND competitors) |
| 5 | **Accreditation Cost** | $ to get and maintain provider approval | Capital requirement |
| 6 | **Online Delivery Allowed** | Can CE be completed online? | Platform fit (TechCelerate is online) |
| 7 | **Competitive Density** | # of existing CE providers, market concentration | Room to compete |
| 8 | **AI/Tech Disruption Opportunity** | Can AI-generated or adaptive content add value? | TechCelerate differentiation |
| 9 | **Price Per CE Hour (market rate)** | What learners/employers pay now | Revenue per credit |
| 10 | **Platform Synergy** | Does this vertical connect to existing Catalyst/CAMEOS/TechCelerate capabilities? | Build leverage |

**Key accreditor note:** CAAHEP and ABHES accredit MA education PROGRAMS (schools). For CE delivery to working MAs, you need AAMA approval as a CE provider (for CMA recertification) or AMT approval (for RMA). These are different from program accreditation. The pathway is: apply to AAMA as an approved CE sponsor → submit individual courses for approval → award CE points.

## STRATEGIC RECOMMENDATION: THE EXPANSION PATH

### The "Synergy-First" Path (RECOMMENDED)

Focus on verticals that leverage existing platform, accreditation, content expertise, and CAMEOS intelligence. Stay in healthcare and adjacent professions.

**Phase 1 (2026):** RT core + Sonographers + Nuclear Med + Rad Therapy = 337K professionals, ONE accreditation (ASRT)

**Phase 2 (2027):** Medical Assistants (743K — AAMA approval) + Pharmacy Techs (472K — ACPE via UofT) = 1.55M professionals, TWO additional accreditations

**Phase 3 (2027-2028):** Surgical Techs + EMS + Imaging Nursing (niche) = adds ~400K, incremental accreditations

**Total synergy path:** ~2M healthcare professionals on one platform.

### The "Maximum TAM" Path (HIGHER RISK, HIGHER CEILING)

If TechCelerate aspires to be a multi-vertical CE platform (the "Coursera of mandatory CE"), the approach changes:

**Step 1:** Build the healthcare vertical (above) to prove the platform model
**Step 2:** ACQUIRE an existing multi-state approved CE provider in a non-healthcare vertical (CPA or legal or insurance) rather than building accreditation from scratch
**Step 3:** Port the AI-powered platform capabilities (adaptive learning, recommendation engine, employer dashboard) into the acquired provider's market

**Acquisition targets would be:**
- Small NASBA-registered CPE providers (CPA market)
- Multi-state approved CLE providers (legal market)
- State-approved insurance CE providers (insurance market)

Each acquisition instantly provides: approved provider status in 30-40 states, existing course catalog, existing customer base. TechCelerate provides: AI platform, adaptive learning, employer dashboard, mobile app — differentiation that transforms a commodity CE provider into a premium offering.

### The "AI Disruption" Path (NICHE PREMIUM)

Instead of going broad, go deep into "AI Literacy CE" across ALL professions. Every profession is being disrupted by AI. None of them have good AI CE:

- "AI for Radiologic Technologists" (ASRT-accredited)
- "AI for Medical Assistants" (AAMA-approved)
- "AI for CPAs" (NASBA-registered)
- "AI for Lawyers" (state bar-approved)
- "AI for Engineers" (state board-approved)

One content theme ("AI in your profession"), packaged for each audience, approved through each accreditation body. TechCelerate becomes the "AI Literacy CE" brand across all professions. Then expand into broader CE for each profession over time.

**Advantages:** Strong brand positioning, premium pricing (AI CE is new and in-demand), less competition (nobody owns this niche across professions), and CAMEOS/Catalyst AI expertise is genuine credibility.

## THE BOTTOM LINE

**If you stay in healthcare (recommended):** The path from 216K (RTs) to 2M+ (full allied health) is clear, leverages your accreditation and content expertise, and builds a defensible healthcare CE platform. Medical Assistants at 743K are your biggest single expansion opportunity.

**If you go multi-vertical:** Don't build — acquire. Buy an approved provider in CPA, legal, or insurance. Port your AI platform capabilities. But don't do this until the healthcare vertical is generating significant recurring revenue (2028+).

**The sleeper play:** "AI Literacy CE" as a cross-professional brand. One topic, many professions. Low content development cost (AI is AI — adapt framing per profession), high demand (every profession is asking for this), premium positioning. This could be Phase 2 alongside healthcare expansion.
