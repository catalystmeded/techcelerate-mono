## QUESTIONS FOR THE CALL (only what the website doesn't answer)

### Question 1: IP Agreement — Does It Cover Self-Learning?
**Why this matters:** The updated IP pricing page says "each submission is a single live lecture activity." If IP only covers live lectures, we need to submit self-learning courses individually at $150–$365 each (for 0.25–3.25 hr courses). This significantly changes our cost model.

**Ask:** "We're planning to submit self-learning activities — online courses that learners complete independently. Can these be submitted under an Institutional Provider agreement, or does the IP agreement only cover live lectures? If self-learning is separate, is there a volume discount or package for multiple self-learning submissions?"

### Question 2: AI-Assisted Content Development
**Why this matters:** We need to know if there's a policy before we invest in the pipeline. We're not asking permission — we're checking for a policy.

**Ask:** "We use AI tools to assist with research and initial drafting of our course content, but all material is reviewed, validated, and approved by licensed radiologic technologists before submission. The listed author is the credentialed RT who is clinically responsible for the content. Does ASRT have any policies or guidelines around AI-assisted content development for CE submissions?"

**If they say "we don't have a policy":** "Great, just wanted to make sure we're being transparent about our process. Thank you."

**If they say "the author must be responsible for accuracy":** "Absolutely — our authors are licensed RTs who review and approve everything. The AI tools are just part of the development workflow, similar to using research databases or editorial assistants."

**If they say "we don't accept AI-generated content":** Don't argue. "Understood, thank you for clarifying." (Then adjust framing internally: AI is a research/drafting tool. The RT authors the course.)

### Question 3: Batch Submission for Self-Learning
**Why this matters:** We want to submit 4–6 courses at once to maximize efficiency and get them all into the review pipeline.

**Ask:** "Can we submit multiple self-learning activities at the same time? For example, if we have 4–6 courses ready, can we submit them all in one batch, or do they need to go one at a time?"

### Question 4: Online Platform Review Requirements
**Why this matters:** ASRT may need to see the course as it appears on the LMS, not just the Word document. This affects our build timeline — the LMS needs to be live before submission.

**Ask:** "For online self-learning activities, does ASRT need to review the course as it's presented on our learning platform — meaning a test login and URL — or is the Word document submission sufficient for review?"

### Question 5: New Sponsor Onboarding
**Why this matters:** We've never submitted before. There may be a first-time application process beyond just submitting courses.

**Ask:** "We're a new CE sponsor — this will be our first submission to ASRT. Is there an initial application or registration process to become a recognized sponsor, or do we simply create an account on the RFA portal and submit our first activity?"

## OUR INTERNAL POLICY: AI CONTENT DEVELOPMENT

Regardless of what ASRT says, this is our standard:

**AI facilitates development. Licensed human experts validate and approve all content.**

- AI tools (Claude) are used for research, drafting, formatting, and compliance checking
- Every course is reviewed, edited, and clinically validated by a licensed RT (listed as author)
- The SME author is clinically responsible for the accuracy of the final approved content
- This is documented in our SME Clinical Reviewer contracts (work-for-hire, clinical accuracy warranty)
- AI is a tool in the development process — like PubMed, Grammarly, or a research assistant
- We do not hide AI involvement, but we don't lead with it either
- If ASRT requires disclosure, we comply transparently

**Framing for any external communication:**
> "Developed with AI-assisted research and writing tools, reviewed and approved by [Author Name], [Credentials]."
